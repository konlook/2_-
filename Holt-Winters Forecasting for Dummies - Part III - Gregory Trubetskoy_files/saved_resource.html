                                                  Expendable Jarad Collier 4 years ago

                                                  Be careful with this approach. You want to fit to the best forecast, not the history. If you want to find the best fit to this historical data, simple exponential smoothing with an alpha of 1 will have 0 error (it just returns the history). I’m guessing that is why your alpha is so close to 1.

                                                  So how do you optimize to the forecast? Typically, you will use holdout data. With 30 historical points with 12 seasonal periods, you could build the model using just 24 points (you need 2 full seasonal periods minimum for an accurate gamma) and forecast the next 6 periods. Then use your SEE optimizer comparing the forecast to the actual 6 points you left out of the model. That way you are fitting to datapoints the model hasn’t seen yet.

                                                  One thing to note, you can do several different holdouts or combinations. For example, if you were primarily curious about 1-month out forecasts, you could generate 6 different 1 month forecasts for the holdout points and average the parameter results. You could optimize to 6-month, quarterly, and monthly forecasts and take a weighted average to find parameters that do well for all periods of forecasting. If you have enough data, you could even test annual forecasts. It really all depends on what you are looking for.

                                                                                                                                      Mnkz Mnkz 6 years ago edited

                                                                                                                                      In my opinion seasonal equation in your footnote is the correct equation. The seasonal equation is the weighted average between the current seasonal index (first term) and the seasonal index of the same season in the previous year (second term). In order to calculate the current seasonal index (first term), you have to subtract non seasonal forecast (level +trend) from current observation or y(t)-[l(t-1)+b(t-1)]. In your equation you are taking weighted average of current (trend + seasonality) and previous (seasonality) which is not correct.
