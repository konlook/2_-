        Rick Deckard 5 years ago edited

        Great series of articles, thanks. However, I believe the difference between smoothing and forecast are not explicitly stated, and can confuse the reader. Other comments mentioned the miraculous appearance of an 8th point using simple exponential smoothing. In Part II we can read that not one, but two points can be forecast thanks to the trend, unlike in simple exponential smoothing where only one forecast point is possible. I believe this is incorrect: I think that no forecast can be made at all for simple ewma, as the trend is missing. And in double ewma you can forcast one point, or more if you then treat the forecasted point as a data point to recompute the next trend.

        In order to remain consistent with the model, in ewma no forecast can be made, and in dewma one point can be forecast. Following this train of thought, I guess to forecast 2 points you need triple non-seasonal ewma (level, trend, and trend acceleration).

            ysamlan 4 years ago edited

            Thanks for the writeup; good explanations here!

            I think there's a bit of a bug in the double-exp smoothing calculation (as evidenced by that smoothed datapoint from the first two datapoints [3, 10] jumping up to [3,17]). It's looking ahead one value too far; when you feed in a series like [1,2,3,4] that becomes very clear as you end up with [1,3,4,5].

            Doing `result.append(last_level+trend)` instead of `result.append(level+trend)` fixes that part.

            Fixing that makes it so that forecasting ends up with a bug, though (since it's *not* looking one datapoint ahead anymore). You can either fix that by not doing the trend recalculation on subsequent passes and just multiplying by how many steps out you are (https://www.itl.nist.gov/di..., or by replacing
            `value = result[-1]` with `value = result[-1] + trend`, which I think essentially acts the same way.
            Again, that behavior is much easier to spot when feeding in a straight-line input; [1,2,3,4,5] should smooth and forecast to [1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,9]... since the initial slope guess of 2-1=1 should never get updated
